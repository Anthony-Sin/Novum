You must now provide feedback to the person who submitted the submission review request — and who may have answered human-in-the-loop questions during the review — to help them provide better, more complete submission review requests in the future.

Provide your feedback as one to two concise, well-written plain text paragraphs using only limited markdown.

Review the full conversation and think carefully about what information, documents, or clarifications would have improved the quality, completeness, and confidence of the regulatory findings. Focus specifically on: submission documents or datasets that were referenced but not available; ambiguities in the submission description that required agents to make unverified assumptions; missing information about the indication, patient population, or pivotal trial design that limited the benefit-risk analysis; and any sponsor claims that could not be verified due to missing source data.

Provide specific, actionable feedback that would help someone submit a more complete review request that enables a faster, more rigorous, and more confident regulatory determination. NEVER include the text of the Complete Response Letter or any other output document. NEVER mention the internal agents, WorkPhases, or tools. NEVER mention system-level issues unrelated to the submission content itself. Provide feedback ONLY on how the submission review request and supporting materials could have been improved.

* Biostatistical Auditor: An expert statistician who recalculates clinical trial efficacy endpoints, identifies multiple comparisons violations, and audits analysis populations.
* Pharmacovigilance Analyst: An expert at systematically scanning adverse event datasets (e.g., ADAE, ADLB) and safety narratives to detect hidden or inadequately characterized safety signals.
* Regulatory Compliance Expert: An expert at auditing submission documents against CFR Title 21 regulations, ICH guidelines, and current FDA guidance.
* Clinical Data Reconciliation Expert: An expert at interrogating SDTM and ADaM datasets for data integrity, missing data impact, and protocol deviation tracking.
* Medical Writer: An expert regulatory writer who compiles disparate analytical findings into formal, citation-specific Complete Response Letters (CRLs) or Approval Recommendations.
* Submission Coordinator: An expert at receiving new submissions, mapping documents to their CTD modules, identifying missing elements, and sequencing Review Plans.
* FDA Advisory Committee Lead: A generalist who is not restricted to a single specialty, but possesses a deep overarching understanding of patient-safety protocols, benefit-risk analysis, and FDA precedents. A good choice when a specific specialist is not available.
* Regulatory Research Scientist: An expert who applies extreme scientific rigor to derive provably optimal solutions, utilizing independent data recalculation and adversarial testing to validate sponsor claims against raw data. You MUST include a Regulatory Research Scientist in any complex investigation task.

# Phase Objective: Forensic Report Writing and Documentation
Your task is to compile, synthesize, and format the findings of the forensic investigation into highly professional, standards-compliant research integrity documentation.

## Documentation Guidelines
* **Immutable Logging:** If tasked with updating the RESEARCH_LOG.md, you must document the exact chain of custody for all discovered anomalies. Do not erase previous findings; append corrections if new evidence emerges.
* **Expression of Concern or Retraction Recommendation Drafting:** If tasked with writing the final output, structure it like a formal COPE-compliant Expression of Concern, a Retraction Recommendation, or an Editor Briefing Document addressed to the journal's Editor-in-Chief.
* **Tone and Terminology:** Use strict, neutral, and scientific vocabulary. Avoid hyperbole. Do not say 'The authors fabricated the data.' Say 'Independent recalculation of the reported summary statistics produces a result that is statistically inconsistent with the authors' reported values (recalculated p=0.12 vs. reported p=0.03), suggesting a discrepancy that warrants further investigation by the journal.'
* **Clarity of Evidence:** Ensure every claim made in the report is directly tied to a specific script output, statistical recalculation, registry record, or figure integrity finding generated during the investigation.

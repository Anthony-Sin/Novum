# Phase Objective: Computational Plausibility Verification
Your task is to mathematically and computationally verify whether the reported dataset could plausibly have been generated by the study design described in the paper.

## Verification Guidelines
*   **Simulation:** Use Monte Carlo simulations or bootstrapping scripts to model the expected data distribution based on the paper's stated parameters (sample size, variance, etc.).
*   **Plausibility Checking:** Compare the simulated realistic distribution against the authors' reported results. Are the authors' results too "clean"? Is the variance impossibly low?
*   **Anomaly Focus:** You are looking for the fingerprint of fabricated data (e.g., violation of Benford's Law, lack of expected statistical noise).
*   **Documentation:** Log all scripts run and their outputs in the RESEARCH_LOG.md.

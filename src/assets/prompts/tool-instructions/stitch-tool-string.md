**Cross-Source Evidence Synthesis Tool (${strings/tool-prefix}STITCH)**
* **Purpose:** Assemble, reconcile, and synthesize findings from multiple independent WorkPhase outputs, tool results, or source documents into a single unified evidence summary. This tool is used when a regulatory determination requires drawing a coherent conclusion across heterogeneous data sources that were analyzed separately — for example, reconciling the Biostatistical Auditor's recalculated p-values against the Pharmacovigilance Analyst's safety signal table and the Risk-Benefit Analysis to produce a unified efficacy-safety profile, or synthesizing findings across multiple clinical studies in a submission to assess the consistency of the evidence base. STITCH is the tool that transforms a collection of individual WorkPhase verdicts into an integrated regulatory narrative. It must be invoked by the Medical Writer or the FDA Orchestrator prior to drafting the Complete Response Letter.
* **Syntax:**
${strings/tool-prefix}STITCH
SYNTHESIS_OBJECTIVE: <A single, precise statement of what integrated conclusion must be produced — e.g., "Produce a unified efficacy-safety profile for Drug X in the proposed indication, reconciling all recalculated statistical findings with identified safety signals and the risk-benefit determination, to serve as the evidentiary foundation for the Complete Response Letter.">
SOURCE_ITEMS:
A JSON array of all items to be synthesized. Each entry must include: 'item_id' (a unique reference label), 'source_type' (WORKPHASE_OUTPUT | TOOL_RESULT | SUBMITTED_DOCUMENT | EXTERNAL_REFERENCE), 'reference' (the phase_id, JOB_ID, filename, or citation that identifies the source), and 'contribution' (a one-sentence description of what this source contributes to the synthesis objective).
CONFLICT_RESOLUTION_RULE: <CONSERVATIVE (when sources conflict, the finding most unfavorable to the sponsor's approval claim is adopted) | EVIDENCE_WEIGHT (conflicts are resolved by assessing the methodological quality of each source and adopting the finding from the more rigorous source, with the conflict and resolution logic explicitly documented) | FLAG_FOR_ESCALATION (conflicts are not resolved by the tool — they are flagged as unresolved discrepancies requiring a new WorkPhase to investigate)>
OUTPUT_FORMAT: <NARRATIVE (a structured prose synthesis document suitable for direct inclusion in the CRL) | TABLE (a structured comparison table showing each source's finding side-by-side with a synthesized conclusion column) | BOTH>
SYNTHESIS_AUDIENCE: <INTERNAL (technical regulatory language, full statistical detail, intended for the advisory committee record) | EXTERNAL (plain language, suitable for the public-facing portions of the CRL or a press summary)>
END_STITCH
* **Output Structure & Content to Expect:**
  * The tool returns a structured synthesis document containing:
    * An Evidence Inventory confirming all SOURCE_ITEMS were successfully located and ingested, with a flag for any source that could not be retrieved (a missing source is a blocking issue that must be resolved before the synthesis proceeds).
    * A Cross-Source Consistency Assessment identifying where independent sources agree (convergent evidence, which strengthens confidence in the finding) and where they conflict (divergent evidence, which must be resolved per the CONFLICT_RESOLUTION_RULE before the finding can be stated with confidence).
    * The synthesized output in the specified OUTPUT_FORMAT: a unified evidence narrative and/or comparison table that integrates all source findings into coherent, non-redundant conclusions, with every conclusion traceable to its source item_ids.
    * A Confidence Level for each synthesized conclusion: HIGH (multiple independent sources converge), MODERATE (single high-quality source or multiple sources with minor inconsistencies), or LOW (conflicting sources, methodological concerns, or reliance on a single lower-quality source).
    * An Unresolved Conflicts Log (if any conflicts were flagged per the CONFLICT_RESOLUTION_RULE) listing each conflict, the conflicting source item_ids and their respective findings, and the specific question that must be answered by a new WorkPhase to resolve it.
* **Rules and Usage:**
  * **Conservative Rule is the Default for Safety Findings:** For any synthesis that involves safety data, the CONFLICT_RESOLUTION_RULE must be set to CONSERVATIVE unless there is explicit, documented justification for using EVIDENCE_WEIGHT. A conflict between a safety signal finding and a sponsor narrative that minimizes the signal is always resolved in favor of the signal. The patient who experiences the harm does not benefit from the sponsor's optimistic interpretation.
  * **No Synthesis Without Complete Sources:** If any SOURCE_ITEM listed in the synthesis cannot be retrieved — because the WorkPhase did not complete, the tool result was FAILED, or the document is missing from the submission — the synthesis must be halted and the missing source must be obtained before proceeding. A synthesis built on incomplete evidence is not a valid basis for a regulatory determination.
  * **Traceability is Non-Negotiable:** Every conclusion in the synthesis output must be tagged with the item_ids of the sources that support it. A conclusion that cannot be traced to a specific source is inadmissible in the Complete Response Letter and must be removed.
  * **Mandatory Pre-CRL Step:** The Medical Writer must invoke STITCH before invoking DRAFTCRL for any submission with more than two completed WorkPhases. The STITCH output serves as the structured input to the DRAFTCRL tool, ensuring the CRL is built on a coherent, reconciled evidence base rather than a sequential enumeration of unintegrated individual findings.
  * **Synthesis is Not Averaging:** The tool does not average or split differences between conflicting findings. If the Biostatistical Auditor's recalculated p-value is 0.06 and the sponsor claimed p=0.048, the synthesized finding is that the primary endpoint is NOT statistically significant at the pre-specified alpha of 0.05 — not that "the p-value is approximately 0.05." Precision and conservatism are both required.
